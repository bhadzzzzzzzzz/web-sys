3 America Countries with Famous and Beautiful Spots!






Canada



1. Niagara Falls



Niagara Falls is a group of three waterfalls at the southern end of Niagara Gorge, spanning the border between the province of Ontario in Canada and the state of New York in the United States. The largest of the three is Horseshoe Falls, which straddles the international border of the two countries. Learn more.




2. Montreal



Montreal is a city in the country of Canada. It is the largest city in the province of Quebec and the second-largest city in Canada. It is the second-largest French-speaking city in the world after Paris. Learn more.




3. Jasper National Park



Jasper National Park, national park in western Alberta, Canada, located on the eastern flank of the Rocky Mountains, north of Banff National Park. Jasper spans 4,200 square miles (10,878 square km) and contains significant active geologic processes, scenic mountains, and diverse animal and plant populations. Learn more.

Brazil



1. Mount Corcovado



Mount Corcovado, sharp rocky peak (2,310 feet [704 metres]), a part of the Carioca Range, overlooking Rio de Janeiro, southeastern Brazil. Mount Corcovado (“Hunchback”) is named for its shape. On its narrow summit towers the imposing statue of Christ the Redeemer, 98 feet (30 metres) tall. learn more.




2. Iguazu Falls



The semicircular waterfall at the heart of this site is some 80 m high and 2,700 m in diameter and is situated on a basaltic line spanning the border between Argentina and Brazil. Made up of many cascades producing vast sprays of water, it is one of the most spectacular waterfalls in the world. Learn more.




3. Ipanema Beach



Ipanema Beach is located in the South Zone of Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Recognised as one of the most beautiful beaches in the world, the sand is divided into segments by lifeguard towers known as 'postos. Learn more.

Mexico



1. Museo Nacional de Antropología



National Museum of Anthropology (Museo Nacional de Antropologia) in Mexico City contains the world's largest collection of ancient Mexican art and also has ethnographic exhibits about Mexico's present-day indigenous groups. The museum has 23 permanent exhibit halls Learn more.




2. Tulum



Tulum has a relaxed ambiance that you will love. This Magic Town of Mexico is on the south side of the Riviera Maya and has become very popular due to its free-spirit tone and its Mayan remains. Get into this pleasing destination in the Mexican Caribbean and enjoy its nature. Learn more.




3. Cenote Calavera



The cenote has a depth of 50 feet and there is a permanent guideline that you must follow at all times. You'll stay underwater for 40 minutes, during which time you'll see a cavern full of rock formations and be stunned by how the glorious light from the small entrances above looks like a skull from below. Learn more.
