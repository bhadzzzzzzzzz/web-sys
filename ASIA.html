3 ASIA Countries with Famous and Beautiful Spots!






Philippines



1. Boracay Beach



Boracay, one of the top Islands in the Philippines, is popular for its beaches and blessed with a long stretch of powdery white sand, crystal clear and azure waters, and a stunning sunset. Boracay is the most iconic island in the Philipines and has won multiple awards in previous years. Learn more.




2. Hundred Island



Hundred Islands National Park is located in the northern Philippines in the province of Pangasinan. There are 124 pristine coral reef islands scattered across an area of 7.12 square miles (18.44 sq km). The islands feature a coral reef foundation with the above ground cliffs being comprised of limestone. Learn more.




3. Puerto Princesa Underground River



One of the river's distinguishing features is that it emerges directly into the sea, and its lower portion is subject to tidal influences. The area also represents a significant habitat for biodiversity conservation. The site contains a full 'mountain-to-sea' ecosystem and has some of the most important forests in Asia. Learn more.

Japan



1. Mt.Fuji



This volcano reaches 3776 meters above sea level and is noted for its steeply sloping (35° near the summit) and symmetric profile. It is often called one of the most beautiful mountains in the world. Learn more.




2. Arashiyama Bamboo Forest



Arashiyama (嵐山, Storm Mountain) is a district on the western outskirts of Kyoto, Japan. It also refers to the mountain across the Ōi River, which forms a backdrop to the district. Arashiyama is a nationally designated Historic Site and Place of Scenic Beauty. Learn more.




3. SanJusangendo Temple



Sanjusangendo, officially called Rengeō-in, is a Buddhist temple of the Tendai sect located in the Higashiyama district of Kyoto. The temple's main hall is famous for housing 1,001 statues of Senju Kannon (1,000-armed Kannon), the goddess of mercy. Learn more.

Korea



1. Nami Island



a half-moon shaped river island located in Chuncheon, Gangwon Province, South Korea, formed as the land around it was inundated by the rising water of the North Han River as the result of the construction of Cheongpyeong Dam in 1944. Learn more.




2. Namsan Seoul Tower



The tower is renowned as a national landmark and for its cityscape views. The 236.7 m (777 ft) tower sits on the Namsan mountain (243m or 797 ft). It attracts thousands of tourists and locals every year, especially during nighttime when the tower lights up. Photographers enjoy the panoramic view the tower offers. Learn more.




3. Hwadam Botanic Garden



Hwadam Botanic Garden is home to 16 themed gardens and around 4,000 species of native and imported plants. Hwadam is not only a tourist spot but also an on-site research facility that aims to restore endangered plants and animals to their natural environment. Learn more.
